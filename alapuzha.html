About Allapuzha :

Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is a coastal city located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is a popular tourist destination, known for its backwaters, beaches, and historic landmarks. One of the main attractions of Alleppey is its vast network of backwaters, which consist of canals, lagoons, and lakes that are interconnected with the Arabian Sea. Visitors can take a leisurely boat ride or houseboat cruise along the backwaters to enjoy the scenic beauty and spot local wildlife such as birds and fish. Alleppey is also home to some beautiful beaches, such as Alappuzha Beach and Marari Beach, which are known for their serene atmosphere and picturesque sunsets. These beaches are ideal for relaxation, sunbathing, and swimming. The city is also known for its historic landmarks, such as the Ambalappuzha Sri Krishna Temple, the St. Mary's Forane Church, and the Mullakkal Temple. These sites offer a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the region and attract visitors from all over the world. Another popular attraction in Alleppey is the Nehru Trophy Boat Race, which takes place every year in August. This event features a competition between traditional snake boats, which are long and narrow boats that are manned by dozens of rowers.
